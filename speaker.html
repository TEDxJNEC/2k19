Meet the Innovators

Dr. Jagannath Dixit

Jagannath Dixit is an Indian professor who was named the Bharatiya Janata Party's brand ambassador for its anti-obesity campaign in Maharashtra. Passion for public service motivated him to develop his own improvised ‘Dixit Diet Plan’ which has helped scores of people resist deadly disorders. Selfless service and dedication defines Dr. Dixit. He also chairs additional responsibilities as Head of Department of Community Medicine at Latur Medical College.

Nikhil Malhotra

Nikhil Malhotra is the creator of Maker’s Lab, a unique Thin-q-bator space within TechMahindra with over 17 years of experience in a variety of technology domains like teaching at premier institutes like RMIT, to working with IBM global services and TechMahindra. Renowned for being an amazing father, Nikhil Malhotra was instrumental in the innovation of the healthcare robot Saarthi. His inputs, along with his little son’s, helped the healthcare robot become a reality which has the potential to help millions, maybe even billions of people all over the world. The initial idea of Saarthi was achieved by Malhotra through the numerous questions asked by his four-year old son, Angad, about what a robot could possibly do in the future.

Dr. B. N. Gangadhar

Prof. B.N. Gangadhar assumed the charge as the Director of National Institute of Mental Health & Neuro Sciences (NIMHANS) on 12 January 2016. Prof. Gangadhar brings over 30 years of extensive experience in the field of mental health, from both clinical and academic perspectives. He obtained his MBBS degree from the Bangalore Medical College in 1978. He was conferred the prestigious Fellow of National Academy of Medical Sciences (FAMS) degree in 2006 and D.Sc. (Doctor of Science) from SVYASA Yoga University, Bengaluru for his research work on Yoga and Mental Health in 2012. With his primary research interests spanning Electroconvulsive Therapy (ECT), Schizophrenia, Community Psychiatry, Public Health and Yoga, he has successfully guided nearly 50 MD students and PhD scholars. He has more than 300 research publications in various national and international indexed journals (H-Index-34, RG Score 44.27) to his credit. He is the co-editor of the Asian Journal of Psychiatry (from 2009) and active member of editorial boards of national and international journals. Prof. Gangadhar is the recipient of the coveted Sir CV Raman Award and Doctors Day Award from the Government of Karnataka. He has also shared over 25 awards with other primary recipients.

Vidisha Baliyan

Vidisha Baliyan (born 1997) is an Indian female deaf model, beauty pageant contestant and a former retired tennis player.[1] She has represented India at the 2017 Summer Deaflympics and also represented India at the 2019 Miss & Mister Deaf World contest. On 19 July 2019, she won the Miss Deaf World Contest and became the first ever Indian to be crowned as a Miss Deaf World. Vidisha was born on 21 August 1997 and was diagnosed with partial hearing impairment at birth. Her parents refused to send her to special school despite the doctor's advice. She attended the MN Public School in Muzaffarnagar for her primary education. She faced discriminations from the other students due to her deafness.She pursued her career through playing tennis at national level. She most notably claimed two silver medals at the 2016 National Games before being called up to take part at the 2017 Summer Deaflympics, her first Deaflympic event. She competed in women's singles and doubles events at the 2017 Deaflympics.

Dr. Ravinder Singal

Dr. Ravinder Singal is a fitness enthusiast who has completed the Deccan Cliffhanger and the toughest triathlon Ironman. He is an IPS officer currently serving as the IGP, Aurangabad. Along with IPS, he is an excellent academician with half a dozen educational qualifications including a PhD, MBA, BE, MA (Human Rights), Diploma (IT) and Diploma (Journalism and Mass Communication). He has proved his exceptional organizational skills by handling the entire management of Kumbh Mela and Guru Ta Gaddi. Besides serving the motherland, Dr. Singal has contributed to the International Community too through the UN Peace Keeping Mission. He is a versatile personality comprising of Ironman, a thinker, a photographer, an author and a motivational speaker. He has been decorated with numerous awards and honours including the President's Medal for Meritorious service, UN Medal for service with UNMIK, Medal for Danish Contingent march at Kosava, Medal on 50th Independence Day, DGP Insignia. In addition he has more than 80 Commendations and Appreciation letters.

Mr. Abraham Pathrose

Consistent journey in the similar field of Heritage Conservation and Tourism development through practice and academics for 23 long years has finally helped Mr. Abraham Pathrose to be titled at various national projects in India which are International and National level Expert titles honoured to him by the concerend apex body and higher authorities of the related projects. Mr. Abraham Pathrose core Idea as a 22 years old student of graduation then and documenting the massive Historical Gates of the town through his dissertation work titled " City of Gates - Aurangabad" by preparing 158 hand made drawings of the existing gates , was to explore ,document and preserve rich treasured heritage sites of India and portray them further to our next generation for making them aware and educate about of Culture and Heritage, to showcase as to where we belong to or rather come from through our ancestral and historical past. Apart from his zeal , he was also blessed with a vision then and had coined a unique title in the books of general knowledge i.e "City of Gates- Aurangabad" which now is being commonly used to address the city.
